# DevPulse AI — Simple App Guide

This guide explains what every tab is for, what you will find inside it, and how to use it.

## Important thing to remember

DevPulse does **not** publish to X or LinkedIn for you.

It helps you research, write, review, plan, and measure content. You always copy the final content and publish it yourself.

---

## Create

### 1. Command center

**What it is:** Your main dashboard and quick summary of the app.

**What is inside:**

- Total number of posts
- Posts waiting for review
- Posts ready to publish
- Recently created posts
- Basic system and AI status
- Quick links to your review queue

**How to use it:**

Open this tab first when you enter the app. Check whether any posts need your review, then open the post you want to work on.

---

### 2. Publishing

**What it is:** The place that tells you what is worth publishing now.

**What is inside:**

- A separate publishing lane for X
- A separate publishing lane for LinkedIn
- The best available post for each platform
- Suggested publishing time
- Reasons why some drafts were held back
- A simple engagement checklist for before and after publishing

**How to use it:**

Check this tab before publishing. Follow the recommendation for X or LinkedIn. If DevPulse says a draft should be held back, read the reason instead of forcing it into the schedule.

This tab recommends what should be published. It still does not publish anything automatically.

---

### 3. Content library

**What it is:** The full history of your generated posts.

**What is inside:**

- Drafts
- Posts waiting for review
- Approved and ready posts
- Posts marked as manually published
- Search and status filters

**How to use it:**

Search for a post or filter by status. Open a post to review its X and LinkedIn text, quality scores, sources, visual content, and publishing actions.

On the post page you can edit, approve, reject, copy, create a visual, and mark the post as published.

---

### 4. Generate

**What it is:** A manual way to ask DevPulse to research and create a new post.

**What is inside:**

- Current generation window
- Quality and cadence information
- Generate or regenerate controls
- Live generation logs
- Recent generation jobs

**How to use it:**

Normally, the scheduled process handles generation. Use this tab when you want to create a post manually or test the generation system.

Start generation, wait for the job to finish, and then open the created post from the Content library.

Do not keep regenerating only to fill every empty window. DevPulse is designed to skip weak ideas.

DevPulse also blocks sources and repository facts that already produced a post. It compares the complete X and LinkedIn drafts with recent content, so changing only a few words does not count as a new post. If the logs say the duplicate guard exhausted the candidate pool, sync the repositories, approve new evidence-backed facts, or wait for genuinely fresh research instead of regenerating the same idea.

## Intelligence

### 5. Research radar

**What it is:** A list of information DevPulse has collected for possible content.

**What is inside:**

- Latest sources
- Source type, title, score, and link
- Recent research runs
- Research logs and errors
- A manual refresh button

**How to use it:**

Use this tab to check where ideas came from and whether research is fresh. Open a source if you want to read the original page.

Use manual refresh only when you need fresh research immediately. Product-based posts may use your approved project facts without needing many outside sources.

---

### 6. Project memory

**What it is:** The trusted memory of your own products and repositories.

**What is inside:**

- Your connected GitHub repositories
- Repository sync controls
- Important project changes
- Suggested project facts
- Approved, rejected, and pending facts

**How to use it:**

Add or sync your repositories. Review the changes DevPulse finds. Approve only facts that are correct and useful.

Approved facts can be used in posts and visuals. Reject routine changes, dependency noise, or anything you do not want DevPulse to mention.

This is one of the most important tabs because better project facts create better product posts.

---

### 7. Analytics

**What it is:** The place to record and compare the real results of your posts.

**What is inside:**

- Impressions and engagement
- Likes, replies, reposts, saves, and clicks
- X and LinkedIn comparisons
- Measurement checkpoints such as 24 hours or 7 days
- Follower checkpoints
- CSV import
- Evidence-based suggestions

**How to use it:**

After publishing a post, return later and enter its results. Use the same checkpoint timing for posts you want to compare.

Also record your current follower count regularly. DevPulse does not guess follower growth from likes or impressions.

The more complete your measurements are, the more useful the recommendations become.

---

### 8. Experiments

**What it is:** A safe place to test one content change at a time.

**What is inside:**

- Active and completed experiments
- Test variants
- Platform selection
- Minimum sample requirements
- Results and winner recommendations

**How to use it:**

Create an experiment when you want to compare something specific, such as:

- Two opening styles
- Text-only versus a branded visual
- Two calls to action
- Short versus detailed posts

Change only one main thing in each experiment. Add real performance results and wait until there is enough data before choosing a winner.

## Growth

### 9. Engagement

**What it is:** A list of useful conversations you may want to join.

**What is inside:**

- X or LinkedIn conversation links
- The author and topic
- A priority score
- A suggested reply
- Reply, dismiss, and outcome controls

**How to use it:**

Open the original conversation, read it fully, and write a useful reply. You may use the suggested reply as a starting point, but edit it in your own voice.

Do not add a product link unless it is truly relevant. The goal is to be helpful and earn profile visits through good conversations.

---

### 10. Distribution

**What it is:** A checklist for everything around manually publishing a post.

**What is inside:**

- Separate X and LinkedIn workflows
- Asset preparation
- Pre-publish engagement
- Manual publishing confirmation
- Comment and reply follow-up
- Performance capture reminders
- Saved audience questions and ideas

**How to use it:**

Open a workflow for the post you plan to publish. Complete the steps in order:

1. Prepare the text and visual.
2. Join a few relevant conversations.
3. Publish manually.
4. Return to answer replies.
5. Record the post results.

Save useful audience questions as future content ideas.

---

### 11. Campaigns

**What it is:** A multi-post story about one product and one goal.

**What is inside:**

- Campaign name and product
- Goal, target, start date, and end date
- Planned story stages
- Calls to action
- Campaign posts
- Goal measurements

**How to use it:**

Create a campaign when one post is not enough. Choose one clear goal, such as awareness, GitHub stars, beta users, or waitlist signups.

Review each planned stage and create posts only when the required evidence exists. Record product results separately from post likes and impressions.

---

### 12. Attribution

**What it is:** A simple way to connect posts with clicks and real product actions.

**What is inside:**

- Trackable links
- Click totals
- Campaign and post connection
- Explicit conversion records
- Funnel summaries

**How to use it:**

Create a tracked link for a repository, product page, or signup page. Use that link in the correct post or campaign.

Later, record real outcomes such as a signup or repository star when you can verify them. DevPulse does not use cookies or build visitor profiles.

Analytics tells you how the post performed on the social platform. Attribution tells you whether people continued to your product.

---

### 13. Weekly review

**What it is:** A weekly summary of what worked, what did not, and what to test next.

**What is inside:**

- Current week compared with the previous week
- Publishing and measurement coverage
- Engagement and follower evidence
- Three decisions: continue, reduce, and test
- PDF and CSV exports

**How to use it:**

Create the review after you have recorded the week’s results. Read the evidence behind each decision.

Approve or reject each decision manually. The review does not secretly change your settings or publish posts.

---

### 14. Execution plan

**What it is:** A seven-day action plan created from the weekly review.

**What is inside:**

- One anchor idea for each day
- Product and content type
- Objective and angle
- Suggested visual and call to action
- Distribution checklist
- Measurement checkpoint
- Calendar export

**How to use it:**

Create the plan after completing the Weekly review. Review every day’s anchor and reject anything you do not want.

Approve the plan when it looks correct. The plan guides future draft generation, but it does not create or publish all seven posts immediately.

---

### 15. 30-day validation

**What it is:** A longer test to see whether your publishing strategy is actually improving growth.

**What is inside:**

- Previous 30-day baseline
- Saved publishing settings
- Checkpoints at the start and on days 7, 14, 21, and 30
- Measurement coverage
- X and LinkedIn results
- Evidence-based recommendations

**How to use it:**

Record your current X and LinkedIn follower counts, then start the study. Keep the main strategy stable during the study.

Capture each checkpoint when it becomes available. At the end, review the recommendations before changing quality limits, cooldowns, or publishing frequency.

The app will not recommend a major change when there is not enough data.

## System

### 16. Schedule

**What it is:** A calendar-style list of upcoming and recent draft windows.

**What is inside:**

- Scheduled time
- Slot number
- X and LinkedIn labels
- Post status
- Links to the related post

**How to use it:**

Use this tab to see when drafts are expected and what is currently pending or ready.

Open a schedule item to review its post. Remember that a scheduled draft time is not automatic social publishing.

Publishing tells you what is recommended for each platform. Schedule tells you when the draft windows are arranged.

---

### 17. Operations

**What it is:** The technical health page for DevPulse.

**What is inside:**

- Database, AI, R2, GitHub, visual renderer, cron, and deployment health
- Recent system runs
- Slow or failed stages
- Missed scheduled jobs
- Recovery actions
- Safe deployment checks

**How to use it:**

Open this tab when generation, repository sync, visuals, or scheduled work fails.

Run the health checks and read the message for the unhealthy service. If a job can be safely resumed, use the recovery control instead of starting everything again.

You do not need to check this tab every day when the app is working normally.

---

### 18. Settings

**What it is:** The main control page for how DevPulse writes, schedules, and brands content.

**What is inside:**

- Timezone
- Quality and novelty limits
- Exact daily generation times
- Number of daily X + LinkedIn draft packs
- LinkedIn posts per week
- Project and content-type cooldowns
- Topics and writing styles
- AI model settings
- Content strategy and product mix
- Visual brand name, handle, colors, tagline, and footer

**How to use it:**

Set your timezone and exact daily generation times first. The default schedule creates five draft packs at 06:30, 12:30, 16:30, 19:30, and 21:30 in your timezone. Keep quality and novelty limits high enough to block weak content. A cooldown value of `0` disables that cooldown.

Review your topics, writing style, product mix, and visual brand. Save your changes before leaving the page.

Avoid changing several strategy settings at the same time. If results change, you should be able to understand which change caused it.

## A simple daily routine

1. Open **Command center** and check the review queue.
2. Open **Publishing** to see what is recommended for X and LinkedIn.
3. Review the selected post in **Content library**.
4. Prepare and publish it manually through **Distribution**.
5. Spend time on useful conversations in **Engagement**.
6. Return later to enter real results in **Analytics**.

## A simple weekly routine

1. Make sure post and follower results are recorded in **Analytics**.
2. Create the **Weekly review**.
3. Approve or reject the three suggested decisions.
4. Create the **Execution plan** for the next seven days.
5. Check **Projects** for facts discovered by the four-hour repository sync, approve only evidence you trust, and check **Operations** for any system problems.

## Common post statuses

- **Draft:** Created but not ready for review or publishing.
- **Pending review:** Waiting for you to check it.
- **Approved:** You accepted the content.
- **Ready:** The post pack is ready to copy and publish.
- **Scheduled:** Connected to a draft window.
- **Posted manually:** You confirmed that you published it yourself.
- **Skipped:** DevPulse intentionally did not use the idea or window.
- **Rejected:** You decided not to use the post.
- **Failed:** A technical step did not finish successfully.
